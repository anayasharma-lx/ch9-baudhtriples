The odd-square method — choose n

Odd numbers — green = perfect square, tap to jump

The nth odd number is (2n−1). When this is a perfect square k², it gives the triple (k, n−1, n). This works because (n−1)² + k² = n². The method generates triples where one leg is just 1 less than the hypotenuse — but not all primitive triples (e.g. 8,15,17 needs a different method).

If (a,b,c) is a triple, so is (ka, kb, kc) for any k — because (ka)²+(kb)² = k²(a²+b²) = k²c² = (kc)². Every scaled triple has a GCD > 1. The primitive triple is the irreducible seed of an infinite family.
